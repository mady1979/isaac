Welcome to ISAAC

Since 2016, the Institute for South Asian Art and Culture (ISAAC LLC) has been dedicated to preserving and sharing the timeless art of Indian classical dance and music.

Located in the Greater Jacksonville area of Northeast Florida, ISAAC offers world-class instruction in classical Indian dance forms to students of all ages and backgrounds. Under the visionary leadership of Sanjukta Chandra, our institute has become a cultural beacon, celebrating and nurturing the profound traditions of South Asian performing arts.

Whether you're pursuing a structured degree curriculum or exploring dance as a recreational passion, ISAAC provides a nurturing environment where tradition meets excellence.

Kathak

Flagship Program

Our premier offering, Kathak is known for its intricate footwork, rapid spins, and expressive storytelling. Available in both degree curriculum and recreational paths.

  • Degree Curriculum Path
  • Recreational Classes
  • All Skill Levels

Odissi

Ancient Temple Dance

Originating from the temples of Odisha, Odissi features graceful postures, fluid movements, and emotive expressions that bring ancient stories to life.

  • Traditional Techniques
  • Temple Dance Heritage
  • All Ages Welcome
Learn More

Bharatnatyam

South Indian Classical

One of the oldest classical dance forms, Bharatnatyam is characterized by precise movements, angular poses, and powerful expressions that convey profound emotions.

  • Classical Technique
  • Expressive Storytelling

Meet Our Director

Sanjukta Chandra

Founder & Artistic Director

Sanjukta Chandra is the visionary founder and artistic director of ISAAC. With a deep passion for preserving and promoting Indian classical arts, she has created a nurturing space where tradition meets contemporary learning. Her dedication to excellence and cultural preservation has established ISAAC as a premier institution for Indian classical dance in Northeast Florida.

Under her guidance, ISAAC has grown into a vibrant community that celebrates the rich heritage of South Asian performing arts while making it accessible to students of diverse backgrounds.
