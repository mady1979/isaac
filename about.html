About ISAAC

Preserving Tradition, Inspiring Excellence Since 2016

Our Story

Founded in 2016, the Institute for South Asian Art and Culture (ISAAC LLC) was established with a profound vision: to create a nurturing space where the ancient traditions of Indian classical dance and music could flourish in Northeast Florida.

ISAAC was born from a deep passion for preserving and sharing the rich cultural heritage of South Asian performing arts. Located in the Greater Jacksonville area, our institute has become a cultural beacon, bringing together students of all ages and backgrounds who share a love for classical Indian arts.

Over the years, we have grown from a small studio into a thriving community of artists, students, and enthusiasts. Our commitment to excellence in teaching, coupled with our respect for tradition, has established ISAAC as a premier destination for authentic classical Indian dance education in the region.

What sets ISAAC apart is our holistic approach to dance education. We don't just teach steps; we immerse our students in the cultural, historical, and spiritual dimensions of these ancient art forms. Through regular workshops, immersion sessions, and performances by renowned artists from India and the United States, we create opportunities for deep learning and cultural exchange.

Our Mission

To preserve, promote, and share the profound traditions of Indian classical dance and music, making these ancient art forms accessible and meaningful to students of all ages and backgrounds.

Our Vision

To be a leading center for South Asian performing arts education, fostering cultural understanding, artistic excellence, and a lifelong appreciation for classical Indian traditions.

Our Values

Excellence in teaching, respect for tradition, inclusive community, cultural authenticity, and dedication to the holistic development of every student.

Meet Our Director

Sanjukta Chandra

Founder & Artistic Director

Sanjukta Chandra is the visionary founder and artistic director of ISAAC, bringing her profound knowledge and passion for Indian classical arts to Northeast Florida.

With a deep commitment to preserving and promoting South Asian cultural traditions, Sanjukta established ISAAC in 2016 with the goal of creating an authentic learning environment where students could experience the transformative power of classical Indian dance.

Her teaching philosophy centers on respect for tradition while embracing contemporary pedagogical approaches. She believes that classical dance is not just about mastering technique, but about understanding the cultural, spiritual, and artistic dimensions that make these art forms timeless.

Under Sanjukta's leadership, ISAAC has grown into a vibrant cultural institution, hosting workshops with renowned artists, organizing cultural events, and building a strong community of dancers and arts enthusiasts. Her dedication to excellence and her warm, inclusive approach have inspired countless students to embark on their own journeys in Indian classical dance.
